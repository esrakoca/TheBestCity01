HISTORICALPLACES OF ORDU



Taşbaşı Kilisesi:Taşbaşı Mahallesi is located in the city center. The church extends in an east-west direction and is in a rectangular plan as a plan. There is a large apse in the east and two small apses in the side. The main site of the church is divided into two rows of three columns and three naves, and the nave is wider than the other naves. The church's semicircular roof is carried by arches supported by columns. Nephtheks in the west are open to the north and south. From today's residue, we understand that there are residences, schools, etc. social places in the north of the church. The church made stone that all of them are smooth in the 19th century. It was used as a prison for a while. It has been restored by the Ministry of Culture.






Bolaman Kalesi:Fatsa is a famous town in the village of Bolaman. The present castle probably remained in Pontus Greeks. The wooden structure, built by the Treasurer family about 200 years ago, is one of the examples of our civil architecture. It is one of the most unique examples of civil architecture in the Black Sea Region. In 1980, the Haznedaroglu Family donated to the Black Sea Technical University on condition that the mansion was restored and opened to the public. But for many years the University had not been able to carry out the restoration, it was transferred to the Ministry of Culture and Tourism. Restoration was completed in 2007.




Yasonburnu Kilisesi:Yasonburnu yarımadası has distance 15km with Perşembe district and 28km with city center. It is within the boundaries of Çaytepe village. 1st degree archaeological, 2nd degree natural SIT area. It is the only peninsula on the Black Sea coast with the church on it. There is Yasonburnu church. The sunrise and sunset can be seen with the naked eye in summer. Yason Burnu Peninsula is where Altinpost Legend (Arganot Legend) passes.





Tozkoparan Kaya Mezarı:The rock tombs from the Hellenistic periods in the Tozkoparan district of Gürpınar village of Unye district and Cevizdere are worth seeing and are still standing. These rock tombs are monumental.





Delikkaya Kaya Mezarı:It is located within the borders of Delikkaya, 10 km away from the city center. It has one-roomed, column-free and double columned entrance.





Unye Kalesi:There is a castle 300-400 m high, 5 km west of the township, which is said to have been a volcano in the very ancient times of its history and that it has extinguished over time, taking its characteristic characteristic today. In the Middle Ages, a number of war facilities were built on the crater of the volcano. The Kalenin platosus has a large pool which appears to have been used during wartime. There is a large cavity, which goes down from the ground, is filled with dipstone and earth, and a ladder of several hundred meters in length. In the moments of war, it is believed that the bridge, which is interrupted by the region, provides the water need from this channel. Both sides of the half-buried gate are decorated with historical and colorful paintings. It is emphasized that the gate was built at the time of the second Mihirdan at a height of 5 km and in a ruined state. However, we believe that objective soundings on the castle and its skirts will illuminate many historical facts that have remained dark.

Perşembe Yaylası:The altitude of Perşembe Yaylası is 1500m. It is in Canik dağları. There are meanders here. Festivity are made here some for too many years ego.



Hoynat Adası:The bird paradise that seagulls and cormorants live is in. It is known as the only place where horned cormorants nest in our country. In olden times, the shipmen used it as a storage and shelter. There are small ramparts and water remains on it. It is within the boundaries of Ramazan village.
